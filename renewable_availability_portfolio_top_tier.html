Executive Overview

Availability was being calculated differently across the portfolio, with each plant following its own contract definitions, exclusions, and measurement logic. This project brought those variations into one structured model that could support both reporting consistency and product scalability.

Contract Analysis

The work started with deep contract review to understand how each plant defined availability, measured performance, and handled exclusions, thresholds, and period logic.

What I Analyzed

  • Reviewed contracts for 50 plants to understand how contractual availability was defined.
  • Extracted formulas, rules, exclusions, and calculation logic across daily, monthly, and yearly periods.
  • Mapped differences between time-based and power-based performance approaches.
  • Identified inconsistencies, ambiguous clauses, and edge cases that affected comparability.

Core Challenge

The challenge was not just technical. Contract language varied in structure, detail, and interpretation, meaning the same portfolio metric could represent different realities across plants. The solution had to preserve contractual intent while simplifying implementation.

Contract Interpretation Edge Case Mapping Cross-Plant Consistency Metric Governance

From Many Variations to One Standardized System

Instead of keeping dozens of custom formulas alive, the project reduced them into a compact set of seven standard formulas, each made flexible through configurable parameters such as calculation period, aggregation level, exclusions, thresholds, and performance basis.

Standardization & Product Design

After identifying the logic patterns, the next step was to translate them into a scalable design that could be embedded into a digital asset management platform without losing flexibility.

01

Pattern Comparison

Compared plant formulas side by side to find overlap, recurring structures, and special-case logic that could be generalized.

02

Formula Consolidation

Reduced the formula universe into seven standardized calculation models that covered the full portfolio with fewer exceptions.

03

Parameter Design

Defined configurable inputs such as period, measurement level, exclusions, and thresholds so each plant could be represented within one shared framework.

04

Product Documentation

Specified how formulas should be configured, implemented, and displayed in the platform across plant, equipment, monthly, and YTD views.

Implementation Thinking

  • Documented formulas in a format usable by product and engineering teams.
  • Defined how users should configure calculations depending on plant contract structure.
  • Suggested output views for portfolio reporting, including by plant, by equipment, monthly, and year-to-date perspectives.
  • Created a logic foundation that supports onboarding of new plants with less manual interpretation.

Strong standardization is not about forcing everything into one rigid rule. It is about designing a system flexible enough to respect contractual differences while still making the portfolio measurable in one language.
